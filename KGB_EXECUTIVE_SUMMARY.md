## THE SCHEME

17 subject accounts operated as a coordinated money laundering network under the name "KGB," aggregating $1.15M from 432 senders via 18,502 P2P payments, then withdrawing $1.20M through 1,697 funds transfers to linked payment sources. The network was established in November 2024 with 16 accounts opened within 6 weeks (Nov 2-Dec 11, 2024), plus one pre-existing account ("Mico Plug") activated in August 2024. Three accounts have prior SAR filings.

## KEY EVIDENCE

**Organized Network:** All 17 accounts share "KGB" account header, opened within 6-week window (Nov-Dec 2024), indicating coordinated establishment of money laundering infrastructure.

**Prior SAR History:** 3 of 17 accounts have previous SAR filings, demonstrating continued suspicious activity despite prior reporting.

**Flow Pattern:** 10.9:1 incoming-to-outgoing ratio. Small P2P payments ($62 avg) aggregated into large withdrawals ($706 avg).

**Network Concentration:** 239 high-velocity senders (10+ transactions each) account for 97% of all incoming money. Top 61 senders alone = 62.4% of total volume.

**Cross-Network Coordination:** 41 senders transacted with multiple subject accounts, proving coordinated operation. One account (C_haexjpygr) uses datacenter IP from Dover with server proxy, indicating technical sophistication.

**Coded Communications:** 71.9% of payment comments are cryptic (number IDs, emojis, single letters) - no legitimate business descriptions.

**Negative Net Flow:** Subjects withdrew $43K more than they received, indicating withdrawal against pending payments or undisclosed funding sources.
